Holly and Neil for President and Vice-President
 

Detailed campaign promises

Here is a list of all the projects and policies we would like to propose and work towards if elected. If you have any questions about any of the following statements, do not hesitate to contact us on any social media platform.

Community initiatives

  • Regulated class group chats for every class
    We were first-year students during the Fall 2020 semester, and we experienced firsthand how confusing and isolating it was to try and find people to collaborate with in your classes! If elected, we plan on starting a network of regulated class group chats on a platform like Discord with categories like program, course, class, and teacher. This way, no student is left behind, and we reinforce the value of community that Marianopolis is so famous for. No need to feel like you’re awkwardly reaching out over Mio, either.

Environment

  • Gardening workshops for all students to participate in voluntarily and a regular composting program at the College for our own garden.
      Montrealers are city folk. It’s a huge part of our identity. For a lot people living in the core of the city or who do not have access to a backyard, it’s hard for them to be introduced to the wonderful world of gardening. Maybe you do have a garden of your own, but you just want to brush up on your skills. In any case, we plan on hosting regular gardening workshops all season round, along with a composting program for the gardens at Marianopolis. Besides, who doesn’t like to get their hands dirty sometimes?
  • Sustainable clothing swap/DIY thrift store to be held at least once a year
      Are you torn between your love for the environment and your love for fashion? As a part of our environmental initiative, we plan on hosting yearly thrifting/clothing swap events. We can definitely promise you won’t have to sift through 80’s prom dresses to find anything decent. Proceeds from the entry of such an event can be given to the eco-friendly organization that you guys care about most!
  • Starting an Ecobrick initiative: the ultimate solution to the world’s plastic problem
      Ecobricks are pieces of thin plastic packaging packed tightly into a plastic bottle. These Ecobricks are super sturdy, and used to construct things like park benches, furniture, and walls. We construct them ourselves and then send them to places that need them. It’s a new way of recycling that we would love to introduce to Marianopolis if elected!

Academic fairness

  • Regulated submission times for all assignments
      Student stress-levels were extremely high last semester, and the fact that different teachers had different deadline times did not make things easier for us. We would like to propose a uniform submission time that all teachers should respect concerning the day their assignments are due. This way, we can worry about the big stuff (like the actual content of our work) rather than how many alarms we should set on phone at night.
  • Making sure learning platforms remain as uniform as possible
      Although we will most likely return to on-campus learning in future semesters, many aspects of online learning are here to stay. According to the mental health survey conducted during the Fall 2020 semester, a large number of students complained about having learning platforms be too numerous and inconsistent. We promise to work with teachers towards standardizing as few of these programs as possible so that you can have all of your work in one place.
  • Regular surveys to be implemented to make sure that teachers are respecting their given ponderation (hours a week of homework)
      Teachers want their students to do their best, that’s just a fact. Sometimes, either too little or too much work for a given topic is assigned, and this makes students very anxious in terms of their time management. For this reason, we plan on improving communication between teachers and students by making teaching surveys a regular part of our semester so that adjustments can be made when needed, not only by the end.
  • Minimizing evaluation overlap during stressful block-end periods (ensuring deadlines are made to be as fair and reasonable as possible during this time with better communication between departments)
      We experience intense levels of stress at the end of each block period before midterm or final exams, and we know you do too. This is why we want to help teachers schedule end-of-block exams much like they schedule their midterms and finals. Communication is key, and we would like to improve the use of a common calendar (like the Mio Calendar) visible to all teachers so they can see where their colleagues are putting their evaluations.

Mental Health

  • Mindfulness workshops to help with anxiety
      Having Buddhist origins, mindfulness is a really useful technique backed by psychologists and psychiatrists alike to improve concentration and reduce anxiety. Teaching mindfulness in our classes has benefits for everyone, whether you’re more stressed or more laid-back of a student.
  • Starting up a hotline/casual chat room for people that do not necessarily qualify for counseling services but still need a pick me up
      Counselling services at the College are amazing, there’s no doubt about it. However, in order to access them, there are often waiting lists based on the severity of your issues. We would like to help implement a more casual approach to counselling services, like chat rooms, for those of us who need support but do not qualify (or do not want) regular appointments with a counsellor.

Social justice

  • Mandatory sexual assault prevention seminars during synchronous classes (1 per semester)
      According to statistics Canada, 1/10 women who attend post-secondary education will be sexually assaulted on campus. This is unacceptable, but better sex education has been proven to be the antidote. We would like to incorporate at least 1 mandatory sexual assault prevention seminar during synchronous classes (or assembly depending on the health guidelines) so that everyone gets to hear what needs to be heard.
  • Make a very visible schedule of protests that people may want to attend, just ask for your event to be included.
      Rally the support you need for your cause! We know activism is a huge part of our lives, and this is why we would like to have a clear schedule of all events or protests to be held that will be filled based on student recommendation.

Transparency

  • An abridged version of congressional activities during meetings to be posted on social media (Instagram, Facebook, etc)
      MSU Congressional activities concern all of us, not just congress members. We want to make meeting details as accessible as possible by posting regular abridged versions of all meetings available not only on the MSU website, but more casually on social media platforms as well.
  • Biweekly general polls and idea submission to be held in order to make real student concerns be heard during our meetings
      In order to be represented accurately, we want your concerns to be heard! A biweekly suggestion period for all of your ideas and issues will be held. Submissions will remain anonymous to avoid conflict. All of these will be brought up during our meetings, and we shall update you on the status of your concerns after every meeting.
  • For anything else, open DM policy, answers to your questions in a maximum of 24-48 hours

Art

  • Art fair/con to be held where students can share their artwork with others and learn how to manage a kiosk.
  • Proceeds from entry for this event can be donated to the charity that students will vote for
      We have so many talented artists here at Marianopolis that deserve to be recognized for the wonderful art they create! This is why we would like to host art conventions where students can sign up to have their own kiosk to sell their art and merchandise to other students. The profits from the entry fees to such an event can be donated to the charity that you guys will vote for!
